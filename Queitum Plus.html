The Prevalence and Impact of Hearing Loss

Hearing loss is a widespread condition, affecting approximately 466 million people globally, and this number is projected to rise significantly in the coming years. It can result from various factors, including age-related degeneration, exposure to loud noises, infections, genetic predisposition, and certain medical conditions. The consequences of hearing loss extend beyond impaired communication; it can lead to social isolation, cognitive decline, and reduced overall life satisfaction.
